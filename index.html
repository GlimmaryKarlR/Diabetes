Pancreatic Kinematics: 4D Morphological Analysis

Diagnostic modeling of T1-weighted mesh arrays (12D Vector Analysis) for early-stage diabetes detection.

🔬 The 12D Volumetric Postulate

By extracting structural mesh data at 60fps from T1-weighted scans, we analyze the micro-deformations of the pancreas. The vector components isolate temporal positioning (t, x, y, z), metabolic proxy intensity (R, G, B), surface morphology (Nx, Ny, Nz), and kinetic strain (d, th). We postulate that localized reduction in kinematic magnitude (d) coupled with increased torsional strain (th) acts as a primary mechanical biomarker for fibrotic stiffening, preceding the onset of clinical diabetes.

Kinematic Velocity

$$v = \frac{d}{\Delta t}$$

Deformation speed indicating tissue elasticity.

Torsional Strain

$$U = \frac{1}{2} \kappa \theta^2$$

Energy stored via rotation, mapping rigid zones.

Metabolic Flux

$$\Phi_E = \iint_{S} \mathbf{F} \cdot \mathbf{\hat{n}} \, dA$$

Intensity projection across surface normals.

📈 Core Kinematic Diagnostics

Micro-Deformation Over Time (1/60s intervals)

Tracks the average kinematic magnitude (d, measured in micrometers) and torsional shift (th, in radians) across the pancreatic head over a 1-second pulse interval. Divergence indicates stiffening.

Energy Proxy vs. Tissue Strain

Correlates the RGB scalar (metabolic proxy) against structural strain (d/th ratio). Bubble size represents surface normal variance. Healthy tissue clusters at high proxy, low strain.

🧬 Diagnostic Segmentation

Regional Biomechanical Health

Compares multiple mesh sectors. A critical drop in localized elasticity in the pancreatic tail often correlates with early islet beta-cell degradation.
